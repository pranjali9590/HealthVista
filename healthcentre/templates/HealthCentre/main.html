  • The number of cases of acute diseases is high in rural areas

    • Possible Reasons: Rural areas are often highly affected by acute diseases due to limited access to healthcare, inadequate infrastructure, lower healthcare education, economic constraints, geographic barriers, and exposure to occupational hazards. Additionally, lack of sanitation, poor nutrition, and cultural factors can contribute to the higher impact of acute diseases in these regions.

  • The number of cases of chronic diseases is high in urban areas

    • Possible Reasons: Urban areas face chronic disease risks due to sedentary lifestyles, poor diets, pollution, stress, and limited healthcare access. These factors lead to conditions like obesity, diabetes, and heart diseases. Solutions involve urban planning, healthcare improvements, and lifestyle changes.
